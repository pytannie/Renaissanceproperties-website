In today's globalized world, many African nationals, particularly Zimbabweans, have migrated to countries such as the UK, USA, Australia, and Asia in pursuit of better economic opportunities. Market Analysis

Target Market

  • Zimbabwean Expatriates: Significant proportion of Zimbabwean nationals live and work abroad in countries like the UK, USA, Australia, and Asia. This demographic seeks stable, high-return investments that are reliable and secure. South Africa offers such opportunities due to its relatively stable economic environment.
  • High-Income Professionals Abroad: Individuals looking to diversify their investment portfolios often view real estate as a reliable vehicle for long-term growth and income generation. South Africa's growing urbanization and demand for housing amplify this potential.
  • Domestic Market: Gauteng Province, a prime location for residential properties, houses industrial hubs, universities, and tourist facilities, creating a steady demand for rental properties.

Market Trends

  • Rising Interest in Affordable Housing: Approximately 76% of residential properties in South Africa are valued under R1.2 million, with government-subsidized housing making up a significant share of this market.
  • Increased Urbanization: The demand for affordable housing is growing due to urbanization, with more than 67% of the population now living in urban areas. Gauteng alone accounts for 38% of the total residential property market value, highlighting its strategic importance for investment.
  • Post-COVID-19 Recovery: The real estate market saw increased activity post-COVID, but rising interest rates in 2023 slowed growth, indicating a shift towards more cautious investment behavior. Urbanization rates in South Africa are expected to rise to 72% by 2030, driving demand for urban housing.
